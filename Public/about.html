About

Our Purpose:

Animals are a beloved part of any family; over 55% of Americans have at least one pet. However, during natural disasters, pets and livestock are often displaced from their homes. In Hurricane Katrina alone, almost 16,000 household pets were missing or required rescue; 85% of these pets never made it back to their owners. Pet survival rates during times of environmental stress are even more concerning due to inferior infrastructure and volunteer relief programs. Because nearly every animal relief organization does not receive federal funding, and because first responders are often overwhelmed with various duties during natural disasters, the issue of rescuing missing household pets persists. This is a preventable problem, which is where PetGet comes in.

Our Solution:

If a PetGet user loses a pet, they can use our friendly interface to report their pet's description, as well as where the pet was last seen. Then, this information is posted on PetGet for all users to see. If a PetGet user finds a pet in distress, they can use our interface to report a description of the animal and where the animal was last found. If the finder moves the pet to a safe location, i.e. a shelter, they can also specify this via our interface. The applications of petget go far beyond natural disasters. Anybody at any time can report a missing pet, which allows for a much greater chance of someone finding a pet/pets and having them returned to their owner. Current animal relief agencies can also use PetGet as an effective medium for locating and rescuing pets more efficiently, as most current pet relief agencies rely solely on law enforcement or conventional scouting.
